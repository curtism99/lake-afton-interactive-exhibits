Kepler's Laws of Planetary Motion

Three scientific planetary laws

  1. The orbit of a planet is an ellipse with the Sun at one of the two foci.
  2. A line segment joining a planet and the Sun sweeps out equal areas during equal intervals of time.
  3. The square of the orbital period of a planet is proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis of its orbit.

Improvement on Copernicus's model

  • Copernicus had hypothesized that all orbits were circular.
  • Kepler, using Tycho Brahe's very accurate observations of the planets, determined Copernicus's model was inaccurate.
  • Newton improved again on Kepler's laws, by introducing the mass of the Sun and the planets as a factor in calculations.

Orbits of Earth satellites can be described with six orbital elements:

  • Semi-major axis (a) - The size of the orbit
  • Eccentricity (e) The shape of the orbit
  • Inclination (i) - The orientation of the orbit with respect to the Earth's equator

Six orbital elements, continued:

  • Argument of Perigee (ω) - Where the low point, perigee, of the orbit is with respect to the Earth's surface
  • Right Ascension of the Ascending Node (Ω) - The location of the ascending and descending orbit locations with respect to the Earth's equatorial plane
  • True/Mean Anomaly (ν) - Where the satellite is within the orbit with respect to perigee
