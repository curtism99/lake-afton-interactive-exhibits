Martian Weather

  • Mars' day lasts 24 hours 39 minutes.
  • Mars' year lasts 668.5 Martian days.
  • The average temperature is -67F but temperatures as high as 80F have been recorded.
  • Dust devils and planet-wide dust-storms are common. The atmosphere is so thin, though, that a dust storm would hardly be noticeable. Sorry Mark Watney.

Olympus Mons

The largest known volcano in the solar system.

Olympus Mons is visible with an average telescope from Earth. It was known to be mountainous long before the first Mars probes were sent to the planet.

Olympus Mons is huge.

However, the slope of this shield-volcano is very shallow (averaging 5 degrees).

The very edge of the slope is bordered with 5-mile high cliffs.

Olympus Mons towers above any mountain on Earth.

The volcano is so large, if you stood on the summit it wouldn't seem like you were standing on a tall mountain.

The slope of the volcano would extend far past the horizon.

Valles Marineris

Valles Marineris is the largest canyon in the solar system. It is a massive, impossible-to-ignore feature of the planet Mars.

At 600 km wide and 8 km deep, Valles Marineris is twenty times as wide and four times as deep as the Grand Canyon.

Valles Marineris would stretch across the entire USA.

Moons

Mars has two very small moons, Phobos and Deimos

While their origin is unknown, the two main theories are that the moons either were captured by Mars from the Asteroid Belt, or that a large impact on Mars ejected material that formed the moons.

Exploration

Mars is the most-explored planet outside of Earth. Entirely populated by robots, Mars has been subject to extensive scrutiny for the last 50 years.

Mariner 4

In 1965, Mariner 4 gave us the first close-up view of Mars. The photos were very poor by today's standards, but they helped paint a picture of the world for the first time.

Viking

In 1976, Viking 1 became the first successful mission landing of a craft on Mars, sending back the first color photos taken from the surface of another planet.

MSL "Curiosity"

The Mars Science Laboratory, otherwise known as the Curiosity Rover, is the most advanced robot ever sent to explore another planet.


Using dozens of instruments including 17 cameras and a laser, the MSL is trying to determine whether life could have ever existed on Mars.

Others

There are currently 13 man-made craft on the surface of Mars. Only two are still operational, Opportunity and Curiosity.

These include Mars 2, Mars 3, Mars 6 (USSR), Viking 1, Viking 2, Pathfinder/Sojourner, Mars Polar Lander, Spirit, Opportunity, Phoenix, and Curiosity (USA), as well as Beagle 2 (UK).

The Mars Climate Orbiter was never meant to land on Mars but after a mixup between English and Metric measurements during development, it crashed into the surface.
